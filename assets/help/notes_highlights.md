# Notes, Highlights & Sharing

Tap any verse in the Reader to select it. A floating action bar appears with everything you can do with that verse (or a range of verses). Tap more verses to add them to the selection, or tap **Deselect** (the ✕) to clear it.

## Highlighting
Pick one of the four highlight colors—**Yellow**, **Green**, **Blue**, or **Pink**—to highlight the selected verses. The color is saved with your data and appears every time you read that passage.

To remove a highlight, select the verse again and tap the **clear** swatch (the crossed-out circle) at the end of the color row.

> Review every highlight you've made from the **Highlights** tool in the side panel, where you can filter by color and jump straight to any verse.

## Notes
Tap **Add Note** to attach a personal note to the selected verse or verses. Notes support plain text and are saved per chapter.

- A small marker appears beside any verse that has a note, so you can spot your notes while reading.
- Open the **My Notes** tool in the side panel to read, print, or delete every note in the current chapter.

## Tagging
Tap **Tag** to file a verse under one or more tags (e.g. `#faith`, `#promises`). Tags are shared across the whole app—the same tag can group verses, notes, journals, sermons, and prayers together. Browse everything under a tag from the **Tags** view, or search for it in the global search bar.

## Compare & Topics
- **Compare** shows the selected verse in *every* translation you have installed, stacked together.
- **Topics** lists the biblical topics a verse belongs to, so you can explore related passages.

## Copy & Share
- **Copy** places the verse text on your clipboard, ready to paste anywhere.
- **Share** offers two options:
  - **Share text** — sends the reference and verse text to any app (Messages, email, notes, etc.).
  - **Share as image** — creates a clean, quotable image card of the verse that's perfect for social media.

You can control how copied and shared text is formatted—whether to include verse numbers and the version abbreviation—under **Settings → Verse Sharing**.
